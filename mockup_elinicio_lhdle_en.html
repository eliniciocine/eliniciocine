The Hour of the Star
Suzana Amaral · 1985 · Brazil · Drama · Fiction
Best Film Award (Havana Film Festival – 1985)  ·  Silver Bear for Best Actress – Marcélia Cartaxo (Berlin – 1986)  ·  Critics' Award for Best Film (Berlin – 1986)

Macabéa is a young orphan who sets out into the world at the age of 19. Illiterate, naive, and a virgin, she leaves Brazil's Northeast to try her luck in São Paulo. Based on the novel of the same name by Clarice Lispector, The Hour of the Star follows Macabéa through her first job experiences, her first love, and the predictions of a fortune teller — a collision of worlds that will seal her fate.

Special screening + talk
Cine Lido, Centro Cultural Bella Época · Mexico City

In partnership with the Guimarães Rosa Institute Mexico of the Government of Brazil and Fondo de Cultura Económica, a special screening of The Hour of the Star, directed by Suzana Amaral, was held at Cine Lido, Centro Cultural Bella Época, in Mexico City. Following the screening, Mexican director María Novaro, alongside writer Brenda Lozano and Brazilian cinema researcher Lilia Lustosa, engaged in a conversation about the connections between literature and film, focusing in particular on the intersections between the work of Suzana Amaral and Clarice Lispector.
